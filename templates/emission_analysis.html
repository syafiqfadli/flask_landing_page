Acionable insights

High-Emission Models: Certain car models were found to have significantly higher CO2 emissions than others. This suggests an opportunity to prioritize engineering and design changes to these models to improve their fuel efficiency and reduce their emissions.

Action: Identify and prioritize design improvements for high-emission car models.


Fuel Efficiency Correlation: There is a strong negative correlation between fuel efficiency and CO2 emissions. The more fuel-efficient a vehicle is, the less CO2 it tends to emit.

Action: Invest more in the development and promotion of fuel-efficient and hybrid vehicles.


Fuel Type Impact: The type of fuel used significantly influences CO2 emissions. Vehicles using gasoline tend to emit more CO2 compared to those using diesel, while hybrid and electric vehicles have the lowest emissions.

Action: Increase the promotion and development of diesel, hybrid, and electric vehicles due to their lower emission profiles. Additionally, investigate the feasibility of alternative fuels like biodiesel or hydrogen.


Emission Standards: Some car models are struggling to meet recent, more stringent emission standards. This could potentially lead to regulatory fines and damage to the brand's reputation.

Action: Focus resources on bringing these models up to the required standards or consider phasing them out in favour of lower-emission models.
